Overall Novelty Assessment

Synthesis of the comparative analysis

MODERATE NOVELTY Based on highest similarity = 85%

The current project (Urban Air AI) demonstrates strong novelty in its specific combination of edge AI/TinyML on ESP32-class microcontrollers for on-device 4-class pollutant source classification (Traffic/Construction/Industrial/Background) with >84% validated accuracy, integrated with a municipal-grade sensor network and health risk prediction layer. While several patents cover IoT-based air quality monitoring and ML-based analytics, the specific integration of quantized TinyML models for real-time source attribution on low-cost hardware, combined with the health outcome correlation layer, represents a differentiated approach. The strongest overlap is with AU2021105263A4 and CN121978286A, but the current project's edge AI source classification capability and health risk modeling provide meaningful differentiation.

Recommendations

Actionable next steps for patent strategy

1
Strengthen claims around the specific TinyML model architecture and quantization techniques for on-device 4-class source classification
2
Emphasize the validated >84% accuracy for source attribution as a key differentiator from general AQI prediction systems
3
Highlight the health risk prediction layer that correlates hyperlocal AQ data with anonymized respiratory health outcomes - this is a unique differentiator not present in most prior art
4
Consider design-around strategies focusing on the specific edge AI implementation details (ESP32-class microcontroller optimization, model quantization methods) to avoid overlap with AU2021105263A4's neural network claims
5
File continuation applications covering the specific sensor fusion algorithms and drift compensation techniques that enable municipal-grade performance at consumer price points
6
Explore freedom-to-operate analysis for the health data correlation layer, as this appears to be a white space in the prior art landscape
7
Consider patenting the specific calibration and validation methodology that achieves R²=0.91 for PM2.5 and R²=0.87 for NO2 with low-cost sensors
