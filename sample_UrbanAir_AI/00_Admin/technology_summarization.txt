Urban Air AI addresses a critical gap in urban air quality monitoring: the inability of existing systems to deliver hyperlocal, real-time, and actionable pollution intelligence at scale. Traditional reference-grade monitoring stations are expensive, sparse, and provide only city-wide averages without identifying pollution sources or health implications. Conversely, low-cost sensor networks suffer from signal drift, cross-sensitivity, and lack intelligent interpretation — rendering their data unreliable for decision-making.

The project's core theoretical innovation lies in applying TinyML (quantized machine learning on resource-constrained edge devices) to environmental sensing. By deploying proprietary quantized models directly on ESP32-class microcontrollers, the system performs real-time drift compensation, multi-sensor fusion, and four-class pollutant source classification (Traffic, Construction, Industrial, Background) at the edge — eliminating the latency and bandwidth costs of cloud-dependent processing. This edge AI approach achieves municipal-grade accuracy (PM2.5: R² = 0.91, RMSE = 3.8 μg/m³; NO₂: R² = 0.87, RMSE = 4.2 ppb) at a fraction of the cost of reference instruments.

A second key innovation is the integration of an epidemiological health risk layer that correlates hyperlocal air quality data with anonymized respiratory health outcomes, transforming raw pollutant readings into actionable health intelligence. The complete edge-to-cloud architecture — comprising custom multi-parameter sensor nodes (PM2.5/PM10, NO₂, O₃, VOC, temperature/humidity) in solar-powered IP65 enclosures, on-device TinyML inference, and a cloud platform for aggregated analytics, interactive dashboards, and automated alerts — delivers a unified system that bridges the gap between low-cost hardware and high-value environmental decision support.
